The Glossary

for Blockchain-isms.

Looking for something in particular?



  • ABI

    Well first of, it stands for Application Binary Interface. An ABI is always linked to an application, and it essentially describes what that application does. It acts like a map of the application, so that other programs can refer to the map rather than searching through the application itself.

  • Address

    Imagine it's your birthday and your friend wants to send you a birthday card. They need to know what your house address is before they can send you the card. Addresses work the same way in Aion. If your friend wants to send you some AION, they need to know your address. Each address is unique, and is 66 characters long. Every single Aion address starts with 0xa. They look like this:

    0xa08091ab0325e384ac45e560d2f85e4b741363aa98881d52d54233a02b33fcaa

    On top of being required for sending an receiving money, addresses are used to store programs. Say I had an application that multiplied two numbers together and gave you the result back. You would need to send two numbers to my application's address.

  • Aion Foundation

    The Aion Foundation (sometimes just called the foundation) is the company that makes everything to do with the Aion blockchain. It is a non-profit, meaning that its goal is not to make lots of money for its shareholders. Aion's goal is to improve the Aion ecosystem.

    The foundation creates and maintains everything on the Aion Networks Github account, and manages the releases of all the Aion projects.

  • Bear Market

    A market that is performing poorly, and is loosing value. People who believe that a market is performing poorly are refered to as bears. This is the opposite of a bull market.

  • Bloom Filter

    It is used for a type of special search for events ; when a transfer triggers an event (eg. event transfer (from, to, value)) a transaction log is generated. any block that has transactions that has transaction logs will generate a bloom which can be used to search for that event.

  • Bull Market

    A market that is performing well, and is gaining value. People who believe that a market is performing well are refered to as bulls. This is the opposite of a bear market. This might have something to do with that big bronze bull on Wall Street, but we're not sure.

  • Byte code

    Computers aren't very good at understanding human language. When they get given a bunch of code, they have to translate it into simple instructions before they can do anything with it. Once they have a step by step guide on what to do, then they can actually run the code.

    Byte code is a bunch of instructions that a computer can understand. It's not designed to be easy for a human to read (it actually looks super confusing), but that's not the point. It's designed to be as efficent as possible for the computer to understand.

    When a program gets compiled with Aion, it gets translated into byte code. This code is then ran by other computers on the network. Again, this code isn't designed to be understood easily by humans. It's designed to be super easy for computers to understand.

  • dApp

    A decentralized application. Regular applications like Netflix and Wikipedia generally has two points of contact, your computer, and their servers.
    This is what happens when you want to view an article on Wikipedia:

    1. You click on a link to view an article.
    2. The Wikipedia receives your request.
    3. It searches for the article.
    4. It send the article back to you.
    5. Your computer shows it on the screen.

    This is how the majority of the internet works. You request something from a server, and the server sends it back to you. There are a lot of problems with this method. For example, if someone wanted you to see an edited version of the article, all they have to do is change it on the Wikipedia server.
    In a decentralized application, the user asks a network of computers for the article, and they collectively respond with all the data. This way the user can be 100% sure that the article they are getting back is completely correct since every single computer on the network has to send the exact same article. If one of the computers tries to change the article, all the other computers will disagree and kick that computer from the network.

  • Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO)

    A business or organization whose decisions are either made by a computer using pre-programmed rules, or by all the members voting on what to do.

    It's pronounced d-aw-w, apparently. Also, this is not to be confused with The DAO.

  • Extra Data

    People on a blockchain can send each other money. This is known as a transaction. In most transactions, the only thing that's moving around is money or cryptocurrency. But sometimes people will add some extra data onto the transaction. This can be something like a reference for the payment, or a thank you note, or just a bunch of random letters and numbers. The purpose of the extra data field is not fixed, and users can do whatever they want with it.

    The special thing about this field is that it's permanent. Once a transaction has happened on a blockchain network, it is public information, and anyone can view the transaction forever. This can be useful for recording events.

    For example: say Alice lent Bob some money to buy a drink. Alice and Bob could make a contract stating that Bob needs to pay Alice $5 in one week. When that time arrives, Bob can create a transaction and sent it over to Alice on the blockchain. To make things clearer, Bob can put "Repayment for the drink last week" in the extra data field. That way, anyone who looks at this transaction in the future can see what it was for.

  • Fiat Currency

    Fiat currency is physical things people use to trade. United States dollars are a fiat currency, as is gold and silver. Cigarettes are often used in prison as fiat currency. It is essentially just something that a collection of people regard as having value.
    For more of a history on fiat currency, check out Extra Credit's History of Money series.

  • Fungible

    Having two things that are exactly the same in value. A single $10 bill has exactly the same value as two $5 bills. It's the same with most cryptocurrencies. My Bitcoin (1BTC) has the exact same value as your Bitcoin (1BTC). Another word for this is interchangeable.
    The opposite of this is non-fungible, which means the opposite. Non-fungible is when two things don't have the same value. My bottle of wine is worth $30, but yours is older and from a more prestigious vineyard, so it's worth $100.

  • Genesis Block

    This is the very first block on a blockchain. When a builder is building a wall, they must place the first brick and secure it in place before moving onto the next brick. When the builder has finished building the wall, every single brick on the wall can be traced back to the original brick.
    In a similar way, every single block on the Aion blockchain can be traced back to the genesis block. Knowing where the genesis block is, or what was on it, isn't really important for day to day Dapp development.

  • Node

    A node is just a computer sitting on a network. In our case, each node is running the Aion kernel. Each node talks to other nodes on the network to do things like run Dapps, or confirm transactions.

  • Nonce

    A nonce is a Number that is only used once. In blockchain, it's used by miners to guess the solution to a math problem. Sounds a bit odd, but let us explain.

    For miners to get a reward, they have to solve an incredibly difficult mathematic problem. This problem has a single answer. The problem is so difficult that it would take an incredibly long time for a single computer to find the solution, but a few hundred computers can usually find the answer pretty quickly.

    Imagine that the miners had to figure out which number, when multiplied by 9, makes 36. A miner could start guessing which number is the correct answer. They'd start at 1, but figure out that 1 x 9 = 9. So then they'd give 2 a go, but find out that 2 x 9 = 18. So they'd try 3, but find 3 x 9 = 27. Finally they'd try 4 x 9 and get the answer 36. This is the answer that the miners were looking for, so that makes 36 the nonce.

  • NRG

    When you go to the post office to deliver a parcel, you need to pay the post office clerk money to deliver the parcel. How much money depends on how big the parcel is, where it is going, and how fast you want it to get there.

    NRG and blockchain networks work in a similar way, except instead of a parcel, there is a transaction. And instead of a post office clerk, there is a blockchain miner. You ask the miner to put your transaction on the blockchain. They look at it, figure out how much work it will take them to do that, and then ask you for a fee. Once you pay the fee, they take your transaction and put it on the blockchain.

    In this case, the fee is NRG, which is a fancy way of saying very small amount of Aion coin.

  • Solidity

    Solidity is a programming language that can be converted into byte code and ran on an Aion node. It was originally created by the Ethereum foundation to run on Ethereum nodes. It looks very similar to JavaScript but contains more features that help it run on a blockchain network.
